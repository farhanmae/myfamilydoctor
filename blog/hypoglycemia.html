Hypoglycemia

Hypoglycemia is a severe condition that results in a low level of blood sugar. Dr.James Sebastian, the best physician in Ernakulam describes hypoglycemia. . A probable cause for the condition is the increase in insulin levels in the body. Insulin is the pancreatic secretion that helps the cells to use glucose, which is the main source of energy. When the blood sugar drops below 70mg/dl, it is considered to be a case of Hypoglycemia. Although mild fluctuations in the blood sugar levels are common, severe drop in the levels can be fatal. It may result in seizures or may even affect the nervous system.

Symptoms:

  • Increased appetite
  • Shakiness
  • Fatigue
  • Anxiety
  • Irregular heartbeat
  • Sweating
  • Drowsiness
  • In severe cases it may result in seizures, blurred visions, loss of consciousness and confusion.

Causes:

A major cause for hypoglycemia is over medication treating diabetes. Other causes may include hormonal imbalances, hepatitis, liver or kidney disorders or excessive insulin production by the body and even extreme drinking habits.

Diagnosis:

There diagnosis is based on the observation of the symptoms and the examination of the medical history and the general intake of medicines and is finally the condition is confirmed upon a blood test. It would also comprise multiple tests being taken while the patient is required to fast.

Prevention:

Continuously monitoring your glucose levels and regulated intake of insulin or diabetes-control medicines is the key to preventing the condition in the case of diabetic persons. Another key preventive measure is to ensure atleast three to four courses of evenly spaced meals and avoid intermittent fasting or skipping any course. A moderate consumption of alcohol is highly advised, especially where one is prone to the recurrence of the condition.

Treatment:

The best remedy in most cases is the intake of foods or drinks rich in carbohydrates. But if the condition persists or is not showing any response to the said treatments, it is essential to immediately seek medical help.
