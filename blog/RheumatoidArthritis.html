Rheumatoid Arthritis

Rheumatoid Arthiritis is an auto immune disorder or connective tissue disorder which can cause joint pain, inflammation, and damage throughout your body. That is, In normal case, our immune system release antibodies attack bacteria from outside. But sometimes, our body mistakenly attacks our body’s tissues. It can also cause medical problems with your heart, lungs, nerves, eyes and skin. Normally,it occurs between the ages between 30 and 80 .Those affected who are below 30 are called Juvenile rheumatoid arthritis and for those who are above 80 is known as rheumatoid arthritis of late onset.

Symptoms

Symptoms commonly affect joints in the hands, wrists, and knees, but can also affect tissues and organs throughout the body including the lungs, heart, and eyes.

  • Pain or aching in more than one joint
  • Tenderness and swelling in more than one joint.
  • Loss of joint function and deformities

Diagnosis

It can be diagonised by blood tests like CRP or ESR. It is confirmed by ACCP or rheumatoid factor. They may also request certain imaging tests, such as an CT-Scan, ultrasound, X-ray, or MRI. Physiotherapy and medication can help slow the disease's progression.

Treatment:

It is never 100% curable but it is treatable. If you don’t get a treatment, its intensity will increase and affect other internal organs of the body. Different disease modifying drugs or steroids are available especially anti-inflammatory drugs. But it should only be taken with the advice of a doctor.
