Prediabetes in Diabetes Care

Prediabetes

Diabetes care is an important aspect that to be taken care of. The blog Prediabetes focuses on the early symptoms of diabetes in human life. Prediabetes is a very common health condition. It can be considered an early warning sign as it signals the possibility of developing diabetes mellitus in the future. In India, over 10 million cases are reported every year.

What is Prediabetes?

Prediabetes is when your blood sugar is higher than normal, but not high enough to be diagnosed as having type 2 diabetes. Prediabetes is also known as early-stage diabetes, borderline diabetes, impaired glucose tolerance, insulin resistance or impaired fasting glucose.

What happens if left untreated?

If people with prediabetes do not get any treatment, they eventually develop type 2 diabetes and other diseases, such as:
  • Heart disease
  • Stroke
  • Kidney damage
  • Nerve damage
  • Foot damage
  • Eye damage
  • Hypertension
  • Dyslipidemia
  • Alzheimer’s disease

What puts you at risk?

Being overweight or obese and having a family history of diabetes are major risk factors of prediabetes. Low levels of HDL cholesterol or high triglyceride levels, having hypertension, having had gestational diabetes, or having PCOS are the other risk factors.

Why is it good news?

Prediabetes is essentially a wake-up call that can stop you from going down a path towards diabetes. By making some immediate and long-lasting changes to your diet and lifestyle, you can reverse prediabetes and prevent type 2 diabetes from developing. Here’s what you can do:

  • Avoid sugary foods
  • Reduce the amount of carbohydrates in your diet
  • Adopt a fibre rich diet
  • Exercise regularly
  • Limit your alcohol intake
  • Quit smoking
  • Lose excess weight
  • Avoid sedentary behaviour and be active
  • Take the medications as prescribed by your doctor

Living with prediabetes

When you are living with prediabetes, your primary objective should be to preventing diabetes mellitus. Remember, prediabetes can be controlled and even reversed through lifestyle modifications. Giving utmost importance to diabetes care is necessary for treating prediabetes and even Diabetes.
