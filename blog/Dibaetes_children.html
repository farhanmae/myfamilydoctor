Diabetes in children

Today most of the parents are facing a serious condition for their children regarding Diabetes. Years ago, Diabetes was only seen in middle aged group but now it has no distinctions. Whether it’s a child, or adult or man or woman, it can happen. But what if it comes to a kid in his/her younger age itself. In this blog, Dr.James Sebastian, one of the senior diabetologists of Kochi, Kerala describes the rise of diabetes in children and the main cause and some measures to prevent diabetes in our younger generation.

Does my child have Diabetes? How to know about it?

Most of us don't control our children's blood sugar. Hence, you probably don't know if it's healthy. Still, there may be signs that your child's levels are getting out of control and you should call your pediatrician. Signs of High Blood Sugar that body Shows:

  • Frequent trips to bathroom: If he/she often has a frequent visit to pee all the time, keep an eye on him, because may be the body is trying to flush out the extra glucose.
  • Extreme Thirst: If he/she is peeing a lot, it may lose their lot of fluids. So they will feel more thirst and tries to compensate that.
  • Weight loss: If the child is having problems to use glucose in a right manner, their body starts breaking the energy or fat resulting in a weight loss.
  • Feeling tired, irritable or moody: If the body is not getting the enough fuels, it will react resulting in tiredness sometimes or an unstable behavior.
  • Vision Problems: High blood sugar can decrease the fluid from eyes, making hard to focus properly

You might assume that low blood sugar is better than high. That's true, but only up to a point: if the blood sugar level drops too low, your child will not have enough energy. If it's too low (your doctor may call it hypoglycemia) your child may have a seizure or even develop brain damage

Signs of Low blood Sugar:

  • A pale or a dry skin
  • Mood swings
  • Confusion
  • Feeling hungry a lot
  • Sweating too much
  • Often gets headaches
Conclusion:

Whether it is a high blood sugar or low, both conditions need proper medications and care. In this hectic world, developing diabetes at a younger age itself needs to be taken care of. Even though we can’t find out a solid reason, but our living style is a common cause for our majority of lifestyle diseases. It’s our duty and responsibility to maintain our children a healthy way of living. Let them live happily without any risk of lifestyle diseases like diabetes. Maintain a proper diet plan for them. Teach them the importance of physical exercise in our life. If there is any need of any medications, consult your doctor .He will guide you how to overcome the Diabetes from a younger age itself.
