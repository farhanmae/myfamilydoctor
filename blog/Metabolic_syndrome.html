What is Metabolic Syndrome?

What is Metabolic Syndrome

Metabolic syndrome is the name given to a group of risk factors for heart disease,diabetes, and other health problems. You may only have one risk factor, but people often have more than one together. If you have at least three of these it is called metabolic syndrome. These risk factors include

  • A broad waist, also known as obesity or "apple shape". Too much fat around the abdominal area is a high risk factor for heart disease
  • Have low HDL cholesterol. HDL is sometimes referred to as "good" cholesterol because it helps remove cholesterol from your arteries.
  • Have high blood pressure if your blood pressure stays high over time, it can damage your heart and lead to other health problems.
  • Have high blood sugar on an empty stomach. Slightly high blood sugar can be an early sign of diabetes.
  • The more factors you have, the higher your risk of heart disease, diabetes, and stroke.

Let’s examine what leads to metabolic syndrome. Metabolic syndrome has several interacting causes:

  • Overweight and obesity
  • An inactive lifestyle
  • Insulin resistance, a situation in which the body cannot produce insulin properly. Insulin is a hormone that helps proper movement of blood sugar through cells for energy. Insulin resistance tends to increase our blood sugar.
  • Age: Your risk increases with age
  • Genetics: ethnicity and family history
  • People having metabolic syndrome also often develops an excessive clotting and inflammation throughout the body. Researchers aren't sure whether these disorders cause or worsen metabolic syndrome.

Who is at risk for Metabolic Syndrome?

The important factors of metabolic syndrome are:

  • Abdominal Obesity
  • A disordered life style
  • Insulin resistance
  • People who are diabetes or have an hereditary traits of diabetes
  • Women with PCOS symptoms
  • People who usually take medications that cause weight gain, changes in blood sugar, cholesterol

How metabolic syndrome can be diagnosed?

  • A high level of triglycerides that is 150 mg / dL or more
  • Low HDL cholesterol-Less than 50 mg / dL for women and Less than 40 mg / dL for men
  • High blood pressure, which is a reading of 130/85 mmHg or higher.
  • High fasting blood sugar that is 100 mg / dL or more.

What are the treatments for Metabolic syndrome?

The main treatment for metabolic syndrome is maintaining heart-healthy lifestyle that includes:

  • A heart healthy diet that limits the amount of saturated and Tran’s fats you eat. Focus on choosing a variety of nutritious foods, including fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean meats.
  • Aim for a healthy weight
  • Coping with stress
  • Get regular physical activity
  • Quit Smoking (or Don't Start If You Don't Smoke Already)
  • If lifestyle changes aren't enough, you may need to take medication. For example, you may need medication to lower your cholesterol or blood pressure.
