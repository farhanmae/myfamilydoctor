Importance of COVID VACCINATION by Physician in Ernakulam

ALL YOU NEED TO KNOW ABOUT THE COVID VACCINATION

Coronavirus disease (COVID-19) is an infectious disease caused by a newly discovered coronavirus. The term COVID -19 has now become an indispensable part of everyone’s life. Dr.James Sebastian, one of the best physicians in Ernakulam is making us aware of COVID 19 and its vaccination.

COVID Vaccination registrations have been opened for all and we don’t see why you are still in contemplation of getting yourself registered. So here are a few points that we would like to share with you, that will help ease the process and negate the unwanted dilemmas in your mind. Covaxin and Covishield are the two vaccines available and which of these will be administered to you is not really your choice, but depends on the availability of the same. Nevertheless, the efficacy of both have been proven to be good. You can get yourself registered for the vaccination via the Arogya Setu App. So, if you haven’t already, go ahead and download the app. Enter the necessary details and find a centre that is nearest to your residence by searching the district or pincode. We suggest district-based search, as the app algorithm works best, in this search mode. Choose the session and date of your choice and set a reminder for the date. Most importantly, turn up at the venue promptly and get your shot.

Remember that there are two shots in a gap of one month and it is necessary that you get the second one to complete the process. After the first shot, you will receive a message with the details to download your certificate of having gotten the first shot. Download the same, as this document is necessary for your second visit. Do not waste time and register for the second one as soon as you get home after the first one, considering the rush and availability of the vaccine doses. Some may experience body pain, swellings etc. That is quite normal.

Take adequate rest, drink enough fluids and approach a physician, if need be. Preferably over the phone as there is no requirement of a hospital visit unless and until advised by your doctor. Effectiveness of the vaccines have been proven and they are safe as well. Even if you get infected after the vaccine, the impact is definitely reduced, ensuring no adverse outcomes and lesser chances of hospitalisation. Vaccines are to be kept on hold for lactating mothers. But there is absolutely no bar on persons with diabetes, blood pressure problems, HIV, kidney problems or cancer survivors. In fact, they should be given priority. There is also no bar on women who are in the course of their menstrual cycle. If you have been COVID infected, ensure that you take the vaccination only after 14 days of your becoming asymptomatic. Also remember that reinfection is a possibility, and therefore continue your precautionary measures in full swing. Pregnant and lactating mothers are required to kindly take the advice of their consulting doctors, regarding the vaccination and also if they are more vulnerable to infections. Meanwhile, stay home, stay safe and do not forget the drill of wearing a mask, social distancing, washing your hands and sanitizing, as needed. The blog by Dr.James Sebastian, best physician in Ernakulam gives us clear-cut information about the vaccination details. He also specifies the importance of hygiene and sanitation in everyone’s life.
