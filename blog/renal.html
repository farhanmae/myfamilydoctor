RENAL CALCULI

This blog by Dr.James Sebastian, the best physician in Ernakulam has explained well about renal calculi or simply saying kidney stones.

Renal calculi better known as kidney stones are mineral and salt-based hard deposits that are formed inside the kidney. Inadequate hydration leads to the urine becoming concentrated and further leading to the crystallization of the minerals. They can even be deposits of calcium or uric acid as well. These deposits can be formed in any part in the urinary tract including the bladder and the urethra. Although, it does not result in any permanent damage per se, this is recognized as one of the most painful medical conditions.

SYMPTOMS

Small stones do not generally show any signs, as it is naturally passed through the urine. However, in other circumstances, the condition is generally marked by severe pain in the back or abdominal region, nausea, vomiting, fever, discoloured or blood-stained urine, and pain while urinating. If left untreated, the condition can lead to urinary obstruction and can result in kidney infection or in some cases, damage of the kidneys.

DIAGNOSIS AND TREATMENT

Symptomatic medical examination, blood tests and x-rays are the most common ways to diagnose renal calculi. If the stone doesn’t naturally pass through urine, various other methods are relied on to achieve the result. Medications are provided to dissolve the stone. In certain other cases, Lithotripsy, or a method where sound waves are used to break down the stone or Urethroscopy where a urethroscope (a camera attached to a wire) is sent up the urethra is used.
