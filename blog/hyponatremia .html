Hyponatremia

The blog which describes Hypernatremia by Dr.James, best physician in Kalamassery is a condition that affects the sodium level in our body. The condition of hyponatremia is a term quite descriptive of the condition. “Hypo” refers to a deficiency, “emia” as a suffix, pertains to blood and “natron” is a term of French origin meaning sodium. Therefore, as the name suggests, Hyponatremia is a deficiency in the levels of sodium in blood, where it drops to a reading below 135 mEq/L. Sodium being an electrolyte keeps the water levels in our body cells in check. In a condition of Hyponatremia, the low sodium levels in blood leads to the swelling of cells due to an incidental increase in water levels. This disruption in the body’s fluid balance is not the only consequence of the condition. It can also affect your other bodily functions as sodium plays a major role in maintaining your blood pressure and the coordinated functioning of nerves and muscles. For the same reason, in some instances it may even result in kidney disease, heart failure etc. Symptoms: Depending on the drop in the sodium levels, the symptoms may be absent, mild or severe. Some of the common symptoms include;

  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Loss of appetite
  • Headaches
  • Memory loss or confusions
  • Muscle weakness or spasms
  • Fatigue
  • Irritability or restlessness
  • Seizures

In severe cases, there can be conditions of cerebral oedema, which is the swelling of brain cells that can result in a coma or even the death of the person.

Causes:

There are no definite set of cause factors for the condition and it could vary from person to person. But some of the commonly observed cause factors include severe diarrhoea, kidney or liver diseases, certain drugs like anti-depressants, diuretics etc, and excess water consumption.

Diagnosis:

Hyponatremia cannot be confirmed, relying merely on the symptoms. In most cases, a blood or urine test would be prescribed, prior to confirming.

Prevention:

Vigilant intake of medications that are likely to cause the condition, and replacing water with electrolytes that have sodium contents in it while engaging in intense physical activities, are some of the ways in which one can prevent a probable condition of Hyponatremia.

Treatment:

Generally, most medical practitioners resort to IV infusions to gradually raise the sodium levels, as a sudden influx can be detrimental. But severe cases of Hyponatremia nay demand emergency treatment to immediately boost the sodium levels.
