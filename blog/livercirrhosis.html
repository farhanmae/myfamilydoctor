Liver cirrhosis

Cirrhosis, also known as liver cirrhosis is impaired liver functioning due to a wide variety of reasons causing destruction and scarring of liver cells and fibroids which leads to hepatic failure.

Causes

At some time ago, the primary causes of the disease included Hepatitis B and C but now, it can be caused by more common reasons like :

  • chronic alcoholism
  • chronic hepatitis
  • non-alcoholic fatty liver disease
  • glycogen storage disease in the liver
  • copper deposition ‌ in the liver called Wilson disease
  • iron deposition in the liver called hemochromatosis
  • inherited disorder named alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency
  • cystic fibrosis and so on...

Symptoms

When signs and symptoms do occur, they may include:

  • Fatigue
  • General tiredness
  • Vomiting
  • Nausea
  • Yellowing of skin and eyes which is called jaundice that occurs due to the rise of bilirubin level in the bloodstream.
  • Dark urine
  • Reddening of palms and skin
  • Spiderlike blood vessels on your skin
  • Easy bleeding tendencies
  • Confusion, drowsiness and slurred speech
  • Ascites, which occurs when fluid accumulates in the abdomen
  • Edema in the limbs

Liver Cirrhosis Complications

Bruising and Bleeding

As we all know bleeding and blood clotting factors are developed by the liver. Cirrhosis causes the liver to slow or stop the production of proteins needed for blood clotting. When blood doesn't clot properly, bruising and bleeding can occur spontaneously. In the early stages it can be curable with the help of a gastroenterologist.

Acute-On-Chronic Cirrhosis

Studies state that the majority of cirrhosis patients are diagnosed with other infections which then leads to multi-organ failure that in most cases is fatal.

Portal Hypertension

Cirrhosis slows down the flow of blood from the intestines and spleen to the liver. As a result, the pressure inside the vein gets increased and the blood may be rerouted to smaller veins that can burst under the increased pressure. In addition, veins in the oesophagus or stomach may become enlarged and reduce the number of WBC and platelets in the blood.

Liver Cirrhosis Prevention

  • Ensure that the diet is balanced and will have to take multivitamins and regular exercise.
  • All patients who are suffering from cirrhosis should abstain from drinking alcohol
  • Prevent chronic hepatitis by avoiding drug abuses, unprotective sex, used needles etc.
  • Hepatitis B can be prevented by taking the vaccination.
