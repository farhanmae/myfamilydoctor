Importance of Vaccination in Adults by Physician in Kochi

Vaccination in Adults

Written By: Anna Mary Theresa | Medically reviewed by: Dr. James Sebastian- best physician in Kochi, Published on: 10th November 2020 All adults need the seasonal vaccine every year. Walk into your best physician doctor in Kochi, communicate your need for immunization. Remember that a little prick today may save your life tomorrow.

We tend to believe in the myth that adults are not susceptible to diseases; that they do not fall ill. But common sense dictates that is not true. The older we get, the more vulnerable we become. It is impossible to ignore the fact that ageing results in decreased resistance power and immunity.

Why is immunization important in adults?

A large number of people seek expert medical care and are hospitalized every year. Majority of these cases involve adults suffering from vaccine-preventable diseases. It is, therefore, highly important to get vaccinated. It will surely help you stay healthy. It will also help shield your family and community from diseases. Wondering why you need vaccines as an adult when you’ve been immunized as a child? Well, the answer to this question is multi-faceted.

  • The protection that childhood vaccinations offer wears off as time passes. So, additional doses of some of the vaccines have become exceedingly essential for your body to defend yourself against serious diseases.
  • Adults are at risk from certain infectious diseases like shingles. Hence, it is recommended that all adults get vaccinated.
  • As a child, some of the newer vaccines may not have been available to you.
  • The germs responsible for viral infections change or mutate over time. For example, the influenza virus mutates as time goes by which means you need an updated vaccination.
  • Your job conditions, travel plans or health problems may put you at an increased risk for various diseases. Therefore, you need to get vaccinated. To learn more about vaccines, click here to watch a short video explaining the why and the when of vaccination in adults. Keep in mind – getting vaccinated is easy. It is a no-brainer. Walk into your doctor’s office today and communicate your need for immunization. Remember that a little prick today may save your life tomorrow.

What are the vaccines recommended for adults in India?

Several diseases prevalent among adults are preventable with the help of vaccines. Without vaccines, you may not only get sick but, in some cases, you also risk death. If you have an underlying condition like diabetes, discuss with your doctor about the vaccines you may need. And always make sure that you stay up-to-date. In India, it is recommended that adults receive immunization for influenza, pneumonia, tetanus, diphtheria, whooping cough, hepatitis B, shingles, etc. A detailed list of vaccinations is given below:

  • Tdap – 2 kinds of Tdap vaccines are available for those people older than 10 years of age. Efficacy of this vaccine is 92%. It is recommended for all adults whose immunization status is not known and for those adults who haven’t received Tdap as a child.
  • Influenza – People with underlying conditions like asthma, heart disease, and diabetes should most definitely get flu shots every year. These people are more prone to serious flu complications, which may result in hospitalization and even death.
  • Pneumococcal – Getting vaccinated is the best way to prevent severe pneumococcal disease. If you are infected, it is very likely that the disease will quickly take a turn for the worse and lead to death. Do bear in mind that not all infections can be prevented with this vaccine.
  • Hepatitis B – It is a serious disease in which the liver is attacked by a virus. It can result in scarring of the liver (cirrhosis), lifelong infection, liver failure, liver cancer, and death. To prevent infection by hepatitis B virus (HBV), it is recommended that people of all age groups get vaccinated with hepatitis B vaccination.
  • Varicella (Chickenpox) – 2 kinds of vaccine for varicella virus is currently available in India. 2 doses are administered in an interval of 4 to 8 weeks. It is recommended that all adults (18 to 49-year-olds) susceptible to infection are vaccinated. It is especially important that the following people take the vaccine: healthcare workers, family contacts of immune-compromised persons, people at a high risk of exposure (example – teachers, international travellers, etc.) MMR – The Measles-Mumps-Rubella vaccine is administered subcutaneously into the upper arm. 2 doses are given at an interval of 4 weeks. It is suitable for adolescents and adults, especially women of childbearing age who aren’t pregnant.
  • HPV – Papillomavirus infections are the precursors to cervical cancer. 2 kinds of HPV vaccines are available. The vaccine has to be delivered before exposure to the Human Papilloma Virus. Therefore, the person has to be immunized before their sexual debut. Age of initiation for vaccination is 10 to 12 years. Catch-up vaccination is advised up to the age of 26 and 45 years.
  • Herpes Zoster – Shingles is a painful rash that presents as an intense burning sensation. The risk of contracting diseases increases as you grow older. Acute infections make chronic problems worse. For example, if you have diabetes and get infected with the influenza virus, then it is highly likely that your immune system cannot ward off the infection. Developing an infection makes it harder for you to keep your blood sugar levels steady. This may lead to serious problems. It is, hence, recommended that diabetic people get vaccinated annually for flu. Another reason for this annual recommendation is due to the fact that viruses mutate and also immunity reduces.
