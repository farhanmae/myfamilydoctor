Dementia

In this blog, Dr. James, the best physician in Kalamassery is making us aware of a severe health condition called Dementia, which has a great impact on our day-to-day life. Dementia, as opposed to general understanding, is not a disease and is different from Alzheimer’s, which is a specific disease forming a type of dementia. Dementia is a generic term denoting a syndrome involving unnatural deterioration of cognitive functions like memory, thinking, orientation, communication etc. There are multiple causes for dementia and Alzheimer’s is one among the many and the most common of them. The condition can sometimes be progressive in nature and could get chronic. Permanent impacts caused by the condition are difficult to be cured completely. Dementia is a condition that is hard on not only the patient, but also the care givers. It can even have psychological impacts on the latter.

Symptoms:

Symptoms may vary in their intensity depending on the stage of the condition. At an early stage, forgetfulness is the most common symptom that is noticed and therefore is often overlooked. More restricting signs are observed as the condition progresses. The advanced symptoms include;

  • Recognition failure
  • Behavioural changes
  • Difficulty in communication
  • Need for personal assistance at all times
  • Difficulty in walking and moving etc

Causes:

Dementia mainly arises out of brain-cell damage that affects the cognitive functions owing to the disrupted communication of the cells. Chronic depression, excessive alcohol consumption, unregulated thyroid etc are some of the factors that cause the deterioration of the brain cells. The associated risk factor intensifies with age.

Diagnosis:

Behavioural and functional changes, medical history and physical examination are the initial steps to diagnosing a condition of dementia. There are no tests that ascertain the prevalence of the condition. Determination is based on the symptoms and an identification of the type is not often possible in most cases as the changes revealed are often common for most types.

Prevention:

Healthy lifestyle is the key to prevent the incidence of dementia. Lifestyle diseases can trigger chances of dementia. Regulated alcohol consumption, regular exercise and nutritious diet are ways to reduce the risks of dementia.

Treatment:

There is no established system of treatment to cure dementia. However, an early detection can halt the syndrome at its initial stage and prevent further worsening of the situation. In case of a failure of the same, treatment of subsidiary health effects and ensuring good physical health, are ways to ease the situation for the patients.
