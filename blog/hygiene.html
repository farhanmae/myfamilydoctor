Importance of Hygiene and Sanitation

This blog by a best general physician in Kochi - Dr. James Sebastian discusses the importance of Hygiene and sanitation. It is rightly said that health is wealth. Out of all the things that one can possess, health is the most significant one. A person’s health is, to a large extent, governed by hygiene and sanitation. These factors need to be taken into account for a person to live in a healthy relationship with his surroundings. Maintaining proper hygiene and sanitation is extremely important primarily because it helps to effectively curb the spread of diseases and infections.

What is hygiene? How important is it in our daily life?

According to WHO, hygiene is defined as “the conditions and practices that help maintain health and prevent the spread of diseases.” A closer examination of the definition reveals that it refers to more than keeping ourselves clean. It also means that we should all avoid practices that lead to bad health, like disposing of garbage in the open roads, etc. Hygienic practices have to be adopted both at a personal and environmental level. And together, we can take a step towards clean and healthy living.

What is sanitation?

All conditions related to public health come under sanitation. It includes adequate disposal of sewage, availability of clean drinking water, etc. Under sanitation comes all the tools and actions that effectively help in keeping the environment clean. Wherever you are in the world, sanitation is a vital part of public health and a country’s development. Sanitation leads to increased life-spans.

What are the various practices we must adopt?

When it comes to personal hygiene, one must adopt practices that contribute to good health. This includes keeping the body clean by regularly taking baths, washing hands, cutting nails, etc. It also involves using clean and boiled drinking water to avoid water-borne diseases. One must also wash fruits and vegetables before using them. Likewise, all food items must be cooked properly. It is essential that one consumes a balanced diet for enhanced immunity to fight against infectious diseases. Good hand hygiene is especially important during the COVID-19 pandemic. It takes less than a minute to wash our hands. By doing so in the proper manner, we are able to prevent the spread of fatal diseases. Many parts of India still remain poverty-stricken. Unfortunately, many people in these areas do not have access to basic sanitation. Facilities like toilets are not available which makes open defecation common practice. Not having facilities for the safe disposal of human urine and faeces can result in a variety of diseases like diarrhoea, typhoid, cholera, and so on. Toilets are vital for clean and healthy communities and also for the social and economic development of our country. Environmental hygiene is of just as equal importance as one’s personal hygiene. Hence, it is essential to keep our houses and surroundings tidy. So, always make sure to properly dispose of your garbage. We must collectively strive to lessen the activities that pollute the environment and make it unfit to live in.

Final Thoughts by Dr.James Sebastian - best general physician in Kochi.

There is one other thing that is just as relevant as the body’s cleanliness and that is the mind and its purity. It is just as indispensable as the rest of it and is something to take care of along with the cleanliness of the body and the surroundings. Everyone must have a positive attitude towards health and hygiene as it boosts one’s self-confidence and image. It creates a better impression among others. A person’s probabilities of success in social settings, interpersonal relationships, etc. are improved by practising personal hygiene on a daily basis. Last but not the least,it is imperative that children are taught the benefits of good personal and environmental hygiene from a very young age so that they learn to keep themselves clean and healthy throughout their lives.
