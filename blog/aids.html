World AIDS Day

This blog indicates A Brief Introduction to AIDS by Dr.James Sebastain - best physician in Ernakulam. World AIDS Day was founded in the year 1987 by James W. Bunn and Thomas Netter at the World Health Organisation in Geneva, Switzerland. It is celebrated every year on the 1st of December. It was considered the first-ever global health day. This day mainly aims at creating awareness among the people about the presence of HIV. In this article, let’s discuss the importance of this day - by Dr.James Sebastian the best physician in Ernakulam, and how it helps people worldwide.

A Brief Introduction to AIDS

AIDS is elaborated as Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome. It is a viral disease caused by a virus called HIV(Human Immuno Virus). It is a life-threatening disease as it damages our immune system directly. It is a communicable disease that spreads from one person to another through the blood. Detecting the presence of this virus in the early stage can help you overcome it. There is no cure for AIDS up to date. However, it can be controlled by medications. The symptoms of this disease include:

  • Rapid weight loss.
  • Recurring fever.
  • profuse night sweats.
  • Extreme tiredness.
  • Diarrhea for more than a week.
  • Sores of the mouth and genitals.
  • Pneumonia.

Why is AIDS Day Important?

Globally, more than 38 million people are living with this deadly virus in their bodies. More than 38 million died due to this disease before it was identified in 1984. Millions still live with this virus without knowing it by themselves. These serious factors had made this day important, unlike any others. The scientific advances had greatly improved. But still, there is no awareness about the seriousness of this virus among people. People lack knowledge about how this virus is caused and spread. This disease remains stigmatized even now. This is why AIDS day is discovered. It aims at spreading awareness and educating people about the importance and the seriousness of this disease.

What Can You Do on AIDS Day 2020?

A red ribbon is commonly worn by people all over the world on this day. This indicated that they are also a part of the global fight against this virus. If you want to contribute from your side against AIDS, wear a red ribbon on this day. Let’s unite against this disease and make humanity win. People infected with this disease are at high risk. It becomes difficult for them to afford their medical charges. A small help from your side can save a day in their lives. Donating to the people infected with AIDS is the best thing one can do on this day. Let’s donate how much ever we could to help them continue their medication. As mentioned above, you can only detect the existence of this disease only after being tested. Bust the myths and come out of the stigma about AIDS – test yourself for this disease. Afterall. AIDS is more likely to be cured in its early stages. Test yourself and also advise others to get tested. Test for AIDS or HIV include the following:

  • ELISA (Enzyme-Linked ImmunoSorbent Assay) – virus detection test.
  • Western Blot – confirmation test.

On this day, let us come together to save lives from falling prey to AIDS as much as we can. Let’s spread awareness regarding the importance of this day to our family members, friends, and all the people we care about. Let’s save humanity from this life-threatening disease. Take the first steps right now to save yourselves and the people around you from AIDS. Let us spread awareness and educate everyone about the cause & spread of HIV.
