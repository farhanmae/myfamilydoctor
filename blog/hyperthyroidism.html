Brief Note about HYPERTHYROIDISM by General Physician in Kochi.

HYPERTHYROIDISM

A condition of overactive thyroid is what is called Hyperthyroidism. The thyroid gland is a butterfly-shaped gland located in our neck region that produces two types of hormones; tetraiodothyronine (T4) and triiodothyronine (T3) that help in regulating the metabolism. Thyroid hormones control the way your body uses energy and it is considered the main hormone that regulates our body’s metabolism. The best general physician Kochi, Dr.James Sebastian gives us an idea of hyperthyroidism in the human body. Excessive production of these hormones is the primary cause and characteristic of Hyperthyroidism. The condition is characterised by high metabolic rates and raced heart rates, often marked by increased blood pressure. The condition generally manifests slowly and is more prevalent among the aged population but in the younger age category, the onset can be abrupt. Similarly, the condition is predominant among the females than males. If untreated, the condition can result in serious complications.

On the other hand, Hypothyroidism is the stark contrast of Hyperthyroidism, where you have an underactive thyroid and the thyroid gland does not produce sufficient levels of the hormones. The effect it has on the body is that, it slows us down, as it affects the body’s metabolism. This condition too, is more common among the female and older population. However, the symptoms of the condition are not seen to manifest at an early stage.

SYMPTOMS

The condition does not result in visible bodily or functional changes till it reaches an aggravated stage. Therefore, the minor irregularities are often disregarded as old-age causes. The nature of the symptoms also differ depending on the age of the patient. The most common of signs are;

  • Fatigue
  • Muscle pains, stiffness and weakness
  • Irregular and heavy menstrual flow
  • Puffiness and dryness
  • Constipation
  • Depression and impaired memory
  • Goiter or enlarged thyroid gland etc.
But hypothyroidism in infants is characterized by jaundice, enlarged tongue, umbilical hernia and hoarseness in their cries. While in the younger and teen population, it is characterized by poor physical and mental development, delayed puberty etc.

CAUSES

Hoshimoto Thyroiditis, an auto-immune disease is one of the main causes for Hypothyridism. The disease produces antibodies that affect body’s tissues, including the thyroid gland. It can also be caused due to thyroid surgeries, radiation therapies or even medications, including over-dosage of medications that were used to treat hyperthyroidism. Hypothyroidism is sometimes consequential to pregnancy, pituitary disorders, and even iodine deficiencies.

RISK FACTORS AND COMPLICATIONS

If left untreated, the condition can lead to some serious health complications ranging from goiter, damage to peripheral nerves, incidence of myxedema coma, infertility and even birth defects to the new-born.

DIAGNOSIS AND TREATMENT

Unwarranted fatigue, paleness or constipation have to be clarified, if it runs for longer than usual. Most importantly, regular check-ups and examinations are highly suggested after a certain age. In the case of hypothyroidism, medical examination for the symptoms and blood tests to check the hormone levels are the commonly undertaken diagnosis methods. There are medications for the instant condition, but it is generally not fully curable, but can be alleviated and kept in control by the said medications. If unchecked, hyperthyroidism is a serious problem that affects our entire organ system.
