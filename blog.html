Hyponatremia

The condition of hyponatremia is a term quite descriptive of the condition. “Hypo” refers to a deficiency, “emia” as a suffix, pertains to blood and “natron” is a term of French origin meaning sodium. Hypoglycemia is a condition of low levels of sugar (glucose) in blood. A probable cause for the condition is the increase in insulin levels in the body.
